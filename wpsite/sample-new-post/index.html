safe and non-toxic cookware for healthy cooking

Oct 4, 2020 | Uncategorized

Modern cookware always allure us and increase our temptation to bring them into the kitchen. As demand increases, many manufacturers started introducing non-stick and ceramic cookware into the market because it is easy to cook food with less oil and dish-washing become total bliss.

But, as you know, with great power comes great responsibility. If you read handling instructions or warning from product description, it has clearly mentioned the hassle that no one go through!

Hence, you will pay the price against your own health. Let me explain, what exactly does it mean and how does it really matter?!

Don’t let beauty of coating allure you!

Yes, you’ve read that right. High-Quality non-stick such as Teflon may be great for cooking but, it has adverse effects on environment and your health as well. Teflon is unstable at higher temperature, it releases toxic fumes. Later, ceramic coating come to rescue. But, it is still a coat on metal which has a high chance to leech chemicals into your food.

Try to distance yourself from Aluminum!

As aluminum is most abundant metal on earth and found it’s way to most of Indian household. During cooking, aluminum leech into your food easily and it’s highly reactive to acidic foods such as lemon, tomato, etc. So, it is not recommended because it is a reactive metal and is linked to Alzheimer’s disease (non-evident).

According to Ayurveda

1. Cook in Brass and Copper for high metabolic rate

Copper rich food helps in increasing metabolic rate of body and collagen. Brass was a common choice among people of small towns and villages. They are useful in detoxification, improving hemoglobin and to aid digestion. But, they are reactive to acidic foods and is not advised to store or cook recipe having tomato, lemon, etc. in it.

2. All natural earthen pots of Clay

Ayurveda suggests to cook in clay because it improves the quality of food and retain nutrients while cooking by leeching healthy minerals such as calcium, magnesium and iron from clay. As cooking in clay pots is a slow process because of high porosity and uniform distribution of heat throughout the body, oil requirement is fairly less in comparison to cook in other type of material.

A word of caution: Avoid glazed pots, which may leech lead or cadmium while cooking and contaminate your food, and of course, Handle with Care!

Safe, Non-toxic and Non-reactive cookware

3. Almighty, Cast Iron

Cast Iron is durable, sturdy and heavy-weight metal which is here to stay long. Though, it does not get heated evenly but, it retains heat for long time. If it is properly seasoned, it may allow you to cook acidic foods for very short time and thus, avoid long-simmered acidic recipe such as tomato sauce.

Another word of caution: Don’t let your cast iron utensil stay wet, otherwise it will get rusty! Season it wil oil to retain non-stick property.

4. 304 Grade (18/8) Stainless steel

Stainless steel is one of the commonly used and popular choice among consumers looking for non-toxic cookware. 18/8 steel is an alloy of 18% chromium and 8% nickel and offer better resistance to corrosion and is non-reactive.

5. Nickel free Stainless steel – FW2 Grade

Nickel free stainless steel is better than 304 grade, developed by Nippon Steel & Sumikin Stainless (NSSC) known as NSSC-FW2 or simply FW2. It contains 0.3% tin, 16% chrome and no nickel, molybdenum and copper content. That’s why, it is highly recommended, safe & non-reactive material for cooking.

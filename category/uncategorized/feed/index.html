In this post, I have listed 4 inexpensive and healthy alternatives to refined oil according to region.

Avoid oil which is refined, hydrogenated or contains Trans-fat!

Unfortunately, most of the popular oil/ghee brands in Indian market are processed and contains trans-fat.

Refined oil is one of the popular choice among Indians to cook food. It is sad, but true! Refined/Hydrogenated oil is highly processed with chemicals and sometimes contains high amount of trans-fatty acids.

There is no evidence which prove that use of refined oil in your meal may increase your risk of chronic diseases, including diabetes, heart-attack, blood-pressure, etc. But, it is certain that Trans Fatty acids and Saturated Fat in high amount will do a severe damage to your body!

If you observe nutritional information of any refined/hydrogenated oil, you can see a clear picture of demon living inside that oil!

I have considered one of the most popular refined oil used in India as an example to demonstrate High Amount of Trans and Saturated Fat in refined oil.

If you continue to use an oil with high amount of bad fats, you may encounter chronic illness in your life soon. You should not pursue the habit of using refined oil in your diet due to their affordability at all!

Oil is an important source of energy in an Indian diet and we should always evaluate our food choices for a better health and fortune!

Choose Regional Cold pressed Oil, as an alternative to refined oil!

Always consider wood/cold pressed oil which is easily available in your region.

In North/North-East, Mustard Oil is easily available and often termed as Kachhi Ghani Sarso/Mustard Oil.

In Rajasthan, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, Sesame Oil would be a better choice of cold-pressed oil.

In middle states of India such as Gujarat, etc., Peanut oil is considered one of good source of healthy fat in diet.

In South India, Coconut oil is popular wood-pressed oil choice and it perfectly blends with region environment as well!

Don’t fall in trap of innovative advertisements and always prefer local/regional unprocessed food choices over modern processed food!

As demand increases, many manufacturers started introducing non-stick and ceramic cookware into the market because they can easily cook food with less oil and dish-washing become total bliss.

But, as you know, with great power comes great responsibility. If you read handling instructions or warnings from product description, it has clearly mentioned the hassle that no one go through!

Hence, you will pay the price against your own health. Let me explain, what exactly does it mean and how does it really matter?!

Don’t let beauty of coating allure you!

Non-stick cookware look beautiful and has smooth/glossy coat on metal which makes it look premium, but, it has adverse effects on environment and your health as well.

These products often label few warnings and hidden dangers that many consumers often overlook! such as Teflon, this coating is unstable at higher temperature and releases toxic fumes.

Any coating such as Teflon or Ceramic, on a metal is very thin and less durable. It is more likely to leech chemicals into your food once coating starts to wear off with time and usage. So, it’s better to avoid them!

Try to distance yourself from Aluminum!

Aluminum found it’s way to most of Indian household because it is most abundant metal on earth and is inexpensive. When you cook in aluminum cookware, it leech into your food easily and it’s highly reactive to acidic foods such as lemon, tomato, etc.

Various studies also suggest that long-term use of aluminum pose threat to brain, kidneys and other vital organs, even under recommended limit, and it is often linked to Alzheimer’s disease.

According to Ayurveda

1. Cook in Brass and Copper for high metabolic rate

Copper rich food helps in increasing metabolic rate of body and collagen. Brass was a common choice among people of small towns and villages. They are useful in detoxification, improving hemoglobin and to aid digestion.

But, Brass and Copper cookware require tinning every 2-3 months (depends on usage) because they are highly reactive metal. Layer of tin prevents direct contact of food and moisture with copper/brass, which ultimately prevents you from food poisoning.

2. Natural earthen pots of Clay

Ayurveda suggests to cook in clay because it improves the quality of food and retain nutrients while cooking by leeching healthy minerals such as calcium, magnesium and iron from clay.

As cooking in clay pots is a slow process because of high porosity and uniform distribution of heat throughout the body. Oil requirement is fairly less, in comparison to cook in other type of material.

A word of caution: Avoid glazed pots, which may leech lead or cadmium while cooking and contaminate your food, and of course, Handle with Care!

Safe, Non-toxic and less-reactive cookware

3. Almighty, Cast Iron

Cast Iron is durable, sturdy and heavy-weight metal which is here to stay long. Though, it does not get heated evenly but, it retains heat for long time.

If it is properly seasoned, it may allow you to cook acidic foods for very short time and thus, avoid long-simmered acidic recipe such as tomato sauce. Foods cooked in this type of cookware, increase the amount of Iron in your food, especially with high moisture content, which can help to reduce Iron deficiency as well!

Another word of caution: Don’t let your cast iron utensil stay wet, otherwise it will get rusty! Season it with oil to retain non-stick property.

4. 304 (18/8) and FW2 (Nickel-free) Stainless steel

Stainless steel is one of the commonly used and popular choice among consumers looking for non-toxic cookware. 18/8 steel is an alloy of 18% chromium and 8% nickel and offer better resistance to corrosion and is non-reactive.

Nickel free stainless steel is better than 304 grade, developed by Nippon Steel & Sumikin Stainless (NSSC) known as NSSC-FW2, or simply FW2. It contains 0.3% tin, 16% chrome and no nickel, molybdenum and copper content.
